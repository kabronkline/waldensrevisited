Expected Schedule

  • 2:00 PM – 2:15 PM — Vote tally completed and winners announced
  • 2:15 PM – 2:30 PM — Election of officers by the new Board
  • 2:30 PM – 3:00 PM — Corporate resolution and meeting adjourned

Purpose of the Meeting

1. Election of Directors — To elect three Directors to the Board of Directors to serve staggered terms of one, two, and three years, per Bylaws §4.01.

2. Election of Officers — The newly elected Board will elect officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer) from among the Directors, per Bylaws §5.01.

How Voting Works

Voting will be conducted by paper ballot. All nominees whose names have been submitted prior to election day will appear on the ballot. Each ballot will also include three blank write-in spaces, allowing voters to nominate and vote for any other qualified and willing candidate directly on their ballot.

With three Board positions open, each voter selects up to three candidates on their ballot. A voter may choose from the pre-printed nominees, write in up to three names, or any combination thereof. The three candidates receiving the most votes when voting ends at 2:00 PM will be elected to the Board of Directors.

You do not need to be present for the entire meeting. Simply arrive during the voting window (1:00–2:00 PM), cast your ballot, and you are free to leave. Results will be published on the Association website after the meeting. Two volunteer vote tally takers from the membership will independently count the ballots, confirm results with each other, and announce the winners.

Authority for This Meeting

This Special Meeting will be called pursuant to Bylaws §3.02 and Ohio Revised Code §1702.17(A)(3), which permits the lesser of 10% or 25 voting members to call a meeting. Three members representing distinct parcels satisfy this threshold.

Proxy Voting

If you are unable to attend in person, you may appoint a proxy to vote on your behalf. To be valid, your proxy must be in writing, signed by the Owner(s), and filed with the Secretary of the meeting before or at the time of the meeting. Per Ohio Revised Code §1702.24, a proxy is valid for 11 months from the date of signature unless an earlier end date is specified.

Return completed proxy forms to: Kabron Kline — 480 Brindle Rd, Delaware, OH 43015 — elections@waldensrevisited.org. Email is preferable. Proxy forms must be received prior to the day of the meeting.
