Board Roles & Responsibilities

Board of Directors

The Board of Directors holds all power and authority of the Association. It consists of three individuals who serve staggered three-year terms—initially one Director receives a one-year term, one receives two years, and one receives three years—so that one seat is up for election each year thereafter. A majority of Directors (two) constitutes a quorum. The Board must meet at least once per calendar quarter.

Eligibility

Directors must be an Owner, the spouse of an Owner, or a principal, member, partner, director, officer, trustee, or employee of an entity that is an Owner. Directors receive no compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred on behalf of the Association.

Powers & Duties

  • Exclusive management and control of the Common Areas
  • Making and enforcing rules and regulations governing property use
  • Imposing sanctions on Owners for violations—monetary fines, suspension of voting rights, and suspension of Common Area access
  • Establishing, levying, and collecting Assessments
  • Obtaining and maintaining insurance for the Association
  • Employing a manager or managing agent
  • Granting easements over Common Areas
  • Taking legal action to enforce the Declaration
  • Borrowing funds and investing excess funds
  • Creating committees and authorizing contracts

Officers

The Association has three required officers—President, Secretary, and Treasurer—all elected by the Board from among its own members. Two or more offices may be held by the same person. Officers serve at the pleasure of the Board and receive no compensation.

President

Presides at all meetings of Members and the Board. Exercises general supervision over the Association’s affairs and performs all duties incident to the office.

Secretary

Keeps accurate records of all proceedings of Members and the Board, including records of names and addresses of Members. Upon leaving office, delivers all books, records, and documents to their successor or to the President.

Treasurer

Receives and safely keeps all money, securities, and intangible property. Disburses funds under the Board’s direction. Keeps correct and complete books and records of account. Presents financial abstracts at annual meetings. Gives bond as required. Upon leaving office, delivers all money and property to their successor or the President.

Sources: 2019 Restated Declaration of Covenants, Easements, Conditions, Restrictions and Assessments for Walden’s Revisited—Sections VII (Membership and Voting Rights) and VIII (Rights and Obligations of the Association); Code of Regulations (Bylaws)—Articles IV (Board of Directors) and V (Officers and Committees).
