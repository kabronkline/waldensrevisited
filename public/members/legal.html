HOA Dissolution & Declaration Validity

Stephen D. Martin, Esq. | Manos, Martin & Pergram Co., LPA

50 N. Sandusky Street, Delaware, OH 43015 | (740) 363-1313

November 2, 2023

Legal opinion addressing questions raised by former board members regarding whether the HOA can be dissolved and whether the Restated Declaration is valid. This correspondence between Scott Gregory, Bobbie Fussichen, Mark Potts, and attorney Stephen D. Martin covers nine key questions about the Association's legal standing.

Key Legal Findings:

  • Restated Declaration is valid. With 21 of 27 lots (77.78%) executing it, the 2019 Restated Declaration exceeded the two-thirds threshold required by Section 1.10 of the original Declaration and is the currently effective governing document.
  • 100% consent required to dissolve. Under Ohio Revised Code § 5312.05(B), termination of the declaration and dissolution of the planned community requires the unanimous consent of all owners — not the 80% stated in the original declaration. That 80% provision is invalid under Ohio's Planned Community Act.
  • HOA is required by law. Because Walden's Revisited has common elements (easements), it is a "planned community" under RC Chapter 5312 and must have an owners association unless and until common elements are terminated.
  • Three steps to dissolve. Dissolution requires unanimous consent to: (a) terminate the easements that constitute common elements, (b) terminate the declarations, and (c) dissolve the HOA. All three could be in a single document signed by every owner.
  • Voting rights. One vote per lot. All lots are entitled to vote regardless of dues payment status.

Ohio Revised Code § 5312.05

Amendments to declaration or bylaws · Effective September 13, 2022 · Senate Bill 61

2022

This section of the Planned Community Act governs how planned communities may amend or terminate their declarations and bylaws. It is the controlling authority cited by attorney Stephen D. Martin establishing that unanimous consent is required to dissolve the Association.

(A) Owners may amend the declaration and bylaws by consent of 75% of the owners.

(B) A vote to terminate the applicability of the declaration and to dissolve the planned community requires the unanimous consent of the owners.

(C) A vote to delete discriminatory provisions requires only a majority vote of the board of directors.

Ohio Planned Community Act

Ohio Revised Code Chapter 5312 · Effective 2010

2010

The Ohio Planned Community Act (RC Chapter 5312) is the state law governing planned communities — residential developments with shared common elements (roads, easements, amenities) and mandatory owners associations. Walden's Revisited, with its common access easements and central pond area, falls under this statute.

Key provisions affecting Walden's Revisited:

  • § 5312.02 — Defines "planned community" and "common elements" (includes easements)
  • § 5312.03 — Requires an owners association for any planned community with common elements
  • § 5312.05 — 75% consent to amend; unanimous consent (100%) to terminate and dissolve
  • § 5312.06 — Authority to levy assessments and enforce liens for unpaid dues
  • § 5312.09 — Court appointment of a receiver if the association is unable to function
