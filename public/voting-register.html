How This Vote Was Validated

The original Declaration (Section 1.10) provides that it may be amended by a two-thirds (66.7%) vote when six or more lots have homes constructed upon them which are occupied. With owners of 21 out of 27 lots signing—representing 77.78% of lots—the Restated Declaration exceeded the required threshold and is the currently effective governing document for Walden's Revisited.

Under the Declaration, there is one vote per lot, regardless of how many individuals are listed as owners on a given parcel. In the absence of a dispute by co-owners, a lot's vote can be made by any one co-owner.

Notes on Parcel Consolidations

The original subdivision contained 27 lots/tracts. Two property owners have since legally consolidated adjacent parcels, reducing the total to 25 current tax parcels:

1. Boles Property (Lot 2/3): Originally platted as Lot 2 and Lot 3. The owners combined them into a single 5.001-acre property. The Delaware County Auditor now recognizes this as a single parcel (20040001060004).

2. Sieger Property (Tract 10/11): Originally platted as Tract 10 and Tract 11. The owners combined them into a single property under parcel 20040001061010.

Important: Parcel consolidation does not affect the covenants. The consolidated parcels remain subject to all easements, restrictions, and obligations under the Restated Declaration. Each consolidated parcel carries one vote in association matters.
